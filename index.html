Stuck in a Rut?

Do you feel like you're trapped in a rut with a near-constant sense of frustration? When you don't know how to get back into the flow of positive energy, you can experience anxiety, depression, sadness, and fear from this sense of being unwillingly anchored to an unhappy life. You don't have to live this way! Take our quiz to figure out why you're stuck and what you can do about it.

When you make daily choices about how to spend your time, who do you think of first?

How do you feel about change?

Do you make time for yourself each day?

How is your activity level?

Can you pinpoint the source of your stagnation?

Take a Quiet Retreat

You're feeling stuck not because of stagnation but because of excessive business with repetitive activities. The best way for you to get out of this rut is to slow down and step back for a moment. Start saying no to commitments that don't make your heart sing and find time for yourself to do some soul searching. Conduct a life audit and analyze what you're giving precious time and energy to. Take a mental health day, turn off your devices, and meditate or journal on your heart's desires so you can return with a refreshed life map.

Make a Change

You feel stuck because you've allowed a powerful area of your life to stagnate. This may be a relationship, job, home, or family dynamic that simply isn't working for you. It's time to cut ties with the anchor that's keeping you mired. Let go of what doesn't serve your highest self and make room for new and wondrous things to enter. If you're not sure where to start, consult a psychic for help identifying the blockages in your life and finding a positive path forward.

Create a Vision Board

You feel both stuck and aimless. You're having trouble identifying your goals, and without a dream to pursue it's impossible to move forward. Get a large piece of paper or create a blank digital canvas and start cutting out and pasting images and words that inspire you. Let your passion and joy carry you along through the project till you have an inspiring image of what you want to do with your life. Then, get to work charting actionable steps to help you achieve it.

Find Support

Though you feel impossibly stuck, you have a good grasp of what's holding you in place. Whether you're in debt, in a dead-end career, or living in a home that needs major repairs, you're quite aware of the problem. You just don't know how to proceed. It's time to reach out for help, though that's not your natural inclination. Search for resources at the library, join a support group, start working with a therapist, or contact a phone psychic. Taking that helping hand will make all the difference.

Get Moving

It's impossible to be stuck and moving at the same time. You've let yourself sink into a place of stagnation and despair, and you won't get out till you get up and go somewhere. Play some powerful music in your earbuds and go for a run, dance it out in your living room, or dedicate yourself to a daily yoga practice. If you have access to a beach or pool, swimming is an excellent way to let the water wash your anxieties away so you can keep your movement going and carry it forward into the process of working toward your other goals.
